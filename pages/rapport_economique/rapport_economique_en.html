Economic report

Sales


Here's a graph showing the evolution of SFR's sales from 2016 to 2021.


Sales fell sharply between 2017 and 2018, dropping by 4.7% from 9.2 billion euros to 8.63 billion euros. This is due to strong rivalry between competitors such as Orange, Free and Bouygues Telecom, who are all experiencing strong sales growth.


After 2018, SFR's sales picked up again, although a slight drop was felt from 2019 to 2020 due to the Covid-19 pandemic.

Mobile operator


SFR covers 22.2% of mobile telephony in France, behind Orange with 35.5% and ahead of Bouygues Telecom with 19.1% and Free with 15.6%.


Mobile telephony refers to objects such as cell phones.

High-speed and ultra-high-speed Internet


SFR covers 21% of the fixed broadband and ultra-fast broadband Internet market, ranking third in France behind Free and Orange with 22% and 39% respectively, but ahead of Bouygues Telecom with 14%.


There are several types of high-speed and ultra-high-speed Internet access, such as cable, ADSL and fiber optics.


FTTH/B subscriber breakdown


SFR has 20% of the optical fiber market, behind Orange and Free with 40% and 24% respectively, but ahead of Bouygues Telecom with 14%.

Outlook


Over the next few years, SFR aims to achieve a number of objectives by 2025, including :


1- Deployment of 5G 3.5 GHZ throughout France:


Indeed, 5G is an inevitable area for ISPs like SFR, which wants to overtake all its competitors such as Orange, Bouygues Telecom and Free. SFR wants to cover 98% of towns with more than 10,000 inhabitants and wants to become the queen of the new mobile generation.


2- Fibre optic deployment throughout France:


SFR's target for 2025 is to cover 90% of French homes that can be connected. The operator estimates that this measure will win 5 million new FTTH customers over 5 years, and the company also hopes to simplify and digitalize customer paths to achieve its goal.
